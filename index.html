Attack Story

BlockSec, a security company, reported an attack on the Euler Finance loan protocol and the theft of $177 million through four transactions. Subsequently, two more transactions were made, bringing the total stolen assets to $197 million. This flash loan attack caused disruption in the operations of 11 other DeFi platforms, including Balancer.

According to the reports by Cryptoslate and BlockSec’s shared data, the hackers have stolen a total of $8.76 million in DAI stablecoin, $33.85 million in USDC, 849 BTC equivalent to $18.5 million, and 85817 ETH equivalent to $13.58 million.

Arkham Intelligence, an analytics company, also confirmed the attack on the Euler protocol. This attack was a flash loan attack. In response to this incident, Euler Finance has stated that they were aware of the vulnerability and the attack by the hackers.

What is Euler

Euler Finance is a protocol that operates as a permissionless lending protocol. Its primary goal is to facilitate lending and borrowing of various cryptocurrencies for users. The UK-based tech startup utilizes mathematical principles to develop non-custodial protocols on Ethereum and other blockchain networks, with a focus on achieving high performance.

Euler Protocol allows users to add different tokens to this platform by using Uniswap's version 3 smart contracts. All assets that have a WETH pair on one side can be added by users of Euler. The Euler platform uses a list of assets in order to reduce users' risk. Users can select any of these assets from the list according to their needs and use them.

What Happend to The Platform

On 13 March 2023 at 08:56:35 AM +UTC, DeFi lending protocol Euler Finance experienced a Flash Loan Attack.

Based on on-chain data analysis, the attacker has successfully executed multiple transactions resulting in the theft of approximately $196 million, making it the largest hack of 2023 thus far. Stolen assets include several million worth of DAI, USDC, Staked Ether (StETH), and Wrapped Bitcoin (WBTC).

The attack was possible due to a lack of liquidity checks in the donateToReserves function of the Etoken. The attacker executed multiple calls with different currencies to generate profit, resulting in a massive loss of $196 million across six different tokens. Currently, the funds remain in the attacker’s account.

1. The attacker first borrowed 30 million DAI through a flash loan from Aave and then deployed two contracts: one for lending and one for liquidation.

2. The attacker then called the deposit function and pledged 20 million DAI to the Euler Protocol contract, receiving 19.5 million eDAI in return.

3. The Euler Protocol allows users to borrow up to 10 times their deposit by calling the mint function. The attacker leveraged this capability to borrow 195.6 million eDAI and 200 million dDAI.

4. The attacker called the repay function using the remaining 10 million DAI borrowed through the flash loan to repay their debt and destroy 10 million dDAI. They then proceeded to call the mint function again to borrow 195.6 million eDAI and 200 million dDAI.

5. The attacker then called the donateToReserves function and donated 10 times the amount needed to repay their debt, sending 100 million eDAI. They then called the liquidate function to initiate the liquidation process and obtained 310 million dDAI and 250 million eDAI.

6. The attacker called the withdraw function and obtained 38.9 million DAI, which they used to repay the 30 million DAI borrowed through the flash loan. They profited 8.87 million DAI from the attack.

The hack is made possible due to the flawed logic its donation and liquidation. Specifically, the donateToReserves needs to ensure the donator is still over-collateralized. And liquidation needs to ensure the *correct* conversion rate from borrow to collateral asset.

TVL (Total Value Locked) is a metric used to measure the value locked into a decentralized finance (DeFi) application or protocol. It is usually calculated by adding the total value of cryptocurrencies or tokens locked in the protocol. TVL is an important metric for DeFi projects as it indicates the amount of capital invested in the application or protocol and can be used to compare the success of projects in the DeFi space.

The TVL of Euler Finance changed after the hack because the hackers were able to access and exploit the smart contract, allowing them to withdraw a large amount of funds. This caused a significant decrease in the total value locked (TVL) in the platform.

Will the stolen funds be returned?!

Euler Labs sent out a strong warning to the attacker who exploited their platform for $196 million by transferring them 0 ETH. In their first message, they asked if the hacker would be open to talking about potential next steps. However, when they did not receive a response, they followed up the next day with an ultimatum: return 90% of the funds within 24 hours or they would launch a $1 million reward for information that leads to the hacker's arrest and the return of all funds. This would mean that the hacker would get to keep $19.6 million. Although this might seem like a good deal, many have noted that the hacker has no incentive to follow through with the agreement.
